What we do:

The Student Climate Strike is a collective of students who fight for climate justice.

"We are fighting for our future and our lives because they are directly threatened by the climate crisis and the ecological breakdown. We are taking action against it because we want to protect the beauty of the earth, the diversity of species and the lives of all beings. Our goal is to overcome the climate crisis and to create a society that lives in harmony with its fellow beings and its environment."

IT'S A MOVEMENT

Our history:

The Évora regional group emerged in February 2019 when some of its members became aware that the international movement #FridaysForFuture and #SchoolStrike4Climate had also been created in Portugal under the name of Greve Climática Estudantil (Student Climate Strike). The internacional movement began in August 2018, after Greta Thunberg and other young activists sat in front of the Swedish parliament to protest against the lack of action on the climate crisis. She posted what she was doing on Social Media and it soon went viral.

Currently, the movement has been making contact with other national and international collectives and joining more fights, more specifically the feminist, the LGBTQ+ community, the anti-fascist, the anti-racist, the cause for the rights of students, and the fair energy transition. Therefore, the movement has been expanding its goals.
